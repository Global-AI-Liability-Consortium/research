# Research

The Global AI Liability Consortium advances AI liability through research that expands understanding, strengthens professional practice, and informs institutional decision-making.

GAILC research examines the legal, regulatory, technical, organizational, economic, and societal dimensions of AI liability, producing enduring knowledge that supports responsible AI accountability.

Together, GAILC's research activities contribute to the world's enduring knowledge infrastructure for AI liability.

> **Liability is where AI disciplines converge.**

---

# Purpose

The Research Program exists to generate rigorous, practical, and interdisciplinary knowledge that advances AI liability as a permanent field of professional and institutional practice.

Research supports the Consortium's Knowledge Hubs, Publications, Standards, Institutional Partnership Program, and broader mission.

---

# Research Philosophy

GAILC research is developed to strengthen understanding rather than advocate predetermined conclusions.

Research should contribute enduring institutional knowledge that remains valuable beyond individual technologies, regulations, organizations, and market cycles.

The Consortium encourages interdisciplinary inquiry that advances both theory and professional practice.

---

# Research Portfolio

The Global AI Liability Consortium develops research including:

## Foundational Research

Research establishing the conceptual foundations of AI liability.

---

## Comparative Research

Comparative analyses of legal, regulatory, technical, and institutional approaches across jurisdictions and sectors.

---

## Applied Research

Research addressing practical challenges faced by organizations deploying, governing, auditing, regulating, or insuring AI systems.

---

## Regulatory Research

Analysis of emerging laws, regulations, standards, and regulatory frameworks affecting AI liability.

---

## Case Studies

Institutional analyses of significant AI deployments, incidents, governance challenges, litigation, enforcement actions, and lessons learned.

---

## Emerging Issues

Research exploring new developments likely to shape the future of AI liability.

---

## Cross-Disciplinary Research

Research connecting multiple AI liability disciplines to strengthen institutional understanding and professional practice.

---
